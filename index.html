AI as operational infrastructure,
not a productivity tip.

The tools here are based on a central hypothesis: AI is most valuable when it is embedded directly into people's workflows as decisions are made, neither a premature shortcut nor a disruptive afterthought. Each tool reflects a real operational problem that product and technical teams face, redesigned with AI as a participant.

I think of Product Managers as Universal Translators. We bridge the gaps between strategy and actual delivery of measurable value, between financial and operational metrics, between customer empathy and technical acceptance criteria, between stakeholders and delivery teams. As I iterate on these tools, I want each interface to streamline the processes of translation and collaboration. Straightforward inputs, in a progressive series of prompts, can get the ball rolling for the creation of robust Product Management assets.

I have spent my career embedded inside delivery teams, working groups with operational leaders, and senior management groups during periods of rapid growth and organizational change, most recently at Salesforce and Delta Dental of California.
